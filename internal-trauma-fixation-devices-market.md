<p><p>Internal trauma fixation devices encompass various types designed to stabilize fractures within the body. Screws are often used to secure bone fragments, while rods provide internal support and maintain alignment in long bone fractures. Plates distribute loads across fractured bones and are typically employed in complex breaks, ensuring stability. Nails, inserted into the medullary canal of long bones, offer strong internal support. Each device plays a crucial role in promoting healing by effectively immobilizing fractured areas.</p></p>
