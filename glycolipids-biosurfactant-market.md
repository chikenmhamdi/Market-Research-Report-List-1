<p><p>Glycolipid biosurfactants, such as sophorolipids and rhamnolipids, are eco-friendly, biodegradable surfactants derived from microbial fermentation. Sophorolipids, produced by yeast like *Starmerella bombicola*, are versatile and used in cosmetics, detergents, and bioremediation due to their mildness and emulsifying properties. Rhamnolipids, synthesized by bacteria like *Pseudomonas aeruginosa*, excel in industrial cleaning, oil recovery, and environmental applications for their strong surface activity and antimicrobial properties. Both types are gaining traction in sustainable markets, driven by demand for green alternatives to synthetic surfactants.</p></p>
