Alkaline batteries utilize a potassium hydroxide electrolyte, providing a longer shelf life and higher energy density, making them suitable for high-drain devices. Acidic batteries, on the other hand, use an acidic electrolyte, typically offering lower performance and shorter lifespan but are often less expensive. Both types cater to different consumer needs, with alkaline batteries favored for modern electronics, while acidic variants remain relevant for basic applications.</p></p>
