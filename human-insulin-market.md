<p><p>The human insulin market can be categorized into insulin analogs and biosimilars. Insulin analogs are genetically modified forms of insulin designed to improve pharmacokinetics, providing better blood sugar control and flexibility in dosing. Biosimilars, on the other hand, are biologics that are highly similar to an existing insulin product, offering potential cost savings while maintaining efficacy and safety. Collectively, these types enhance treatment options for diabetes patients, addressing diverse needs and preferences in insulin management.</p></p>
