<p><p>Li-ion batteries have largely replaced older technologies like Nickel-Cadmium (NiCd) and Nickel Metal Hydride (NiMH) in mobile phones due to their higher energy density, lighter weight, and lower self-discharge rates. NiCd batteries, known for their durability, suffer from memory effect and environmental concerns. NiMH batteries offer better capacity than NiCd but are still heavier and less efficient than Li-ion. As technology advances, Li-ion remains the preferred choice in the mobile phone market for its overall performance and longevity.</p></p>
